# Phase 1 — Player & Game Feeling

**Goal:** Establish who the player is and what emotional experience the game targets.

---

## Questions to Work Through

Ask these one at a time. You don't need to ask all of them — stop when you have enough to fill
in the Phase 1 summary.

### 1a. Game Feeling
> "What feeling do you want players to walk away with?"

Good answers: triumphant, tense, curious, delighted, clever, terrified, relaxed, competitive.
If vague ("fun" or "good"), push back: "Fun is our goal, but what *kind* of fun? Think about
a moment in a game that made you feel something strong — what was that feeling?"

### 1b. Types of Fun (from MDA Framework)
After they name a feeling, map it to the 8 Kinds of Fun and confirm:

| Type | Core Appeal |
|------|-------------|
| **Sensation** | Sights, sounds, touch — raw sensory pleasure |
| **Fellowship** | Social bonds, cooperation, shared experience |
| **Challenge** | Mastery, skill, overcoming difficulty |
| **Fantasy** | Role-playing, living as someone/something else |
| **Narrative** | Story, unfolding events, emotional arcs |
| **Discovery** | Exploration, hidden things, surprises |
| **Expression** | Creativity, individuality, self-expression |
| **Submission** | Relaxation, low-effort engagement, routine |

Ask: "Based on that feeling, your game seems to lean toward [X type]. Does that sound right?
And is there a second type you'd want to blend in?"

Note: Some types blend well (Sensation + Fantasy + Narrative = immersive); some don't
(Challenge + Narrative can conflict — high difficulty breaks story flow).

### 1c. Target Player
> "Who do you imagine playing this? Casual players, competitive players, people who love
> stories, kids, your friend group — who's this for?"

This will inform complexity, depth, and pacing decisions later.

### 1d. Solo or Multiplayer
> "Do you see this as a solo experience, something multiplayer, or could it be both?"

---

## Phase 1 Summary Block

Once you have their answers, write:

```
📋 PHASE 1 COMPLETE — Player & Game Feeling
• Game feeling: [e.g., tense triumph]
• Primary type of fun: [e.g., Challenge]
• Secondary type of fun: [e.g., Fellowship]
• Target player: [e.g., competitive friends, ages 16+]
• Solo/Multiplayer: [e.g., 2–4 players]
```

Then say: "Great. Now let's figure out what players are actually trying to *do* — the goal."
