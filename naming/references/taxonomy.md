# Name Taxonomy Reference

A condensed reference for the five naming categories, with representative examples and key patterns. Use during Phase 2 (Exploration) to broaden generation, and Phase 3 (Distillation) to categorize candidates.

---

## Descriptive
*Says what it is and does. Utilitarian, reliable, limited.*

**Pros:** Immediately clear, easy to understand
**Cons:** Hard to trademark, limits expansion, commoditizes the offering

**Subcategories:**
- **Initialisms/Acronyms** — BMW, NASA, UNESCO, AOL, AT&T
- **Numbers/Symbols** — 23andMe, 8 Sleep, A24, a16z, 3M
- **Compound** — Dropbox, Facebook, LinkedIn, Snapchat, YouTube, WhatsApp, ProductHunt
- **Generic Descriptor** — General Motors, Hotel Tonight, Standard Hotel, Ace Hotel, Airbus
- **Authoritative/Utilitarian** — Best Buy, Whole Foods, Madewell, The Honest Company
- **Synecdoche** (based on what it's made of) — Glassdoor, Blue Bottle, Chainlink, Steelcase
- **Prefix/Suffix Trends** — Airtable/Airbnb; Coinbase/Supabase; SpaceX/X.com

---

## Experiential
*Conveys the emotional outcome, the benefit, or the experience rather than the product.*

**Pros:** Emotionally resonant, memorable, suggests benefits not features
**Cons:** Can feel abstract, may not age well if the product pivots

**Subcategories:**
- **Emotional** — Tinder, SoulCycle, Outdoor Voices, Seamless, ZipCar, Canopy
- **Sensory/Onomatopoeia** — Coca-Cola, TikTok, Twitter, Ring, SONOS, Whoop, Zapier
- **Action/Statement** — Zoom, TurboTax, Seamless, The Boring Company, iHeartRadio
- **Edge/Attitude** — Red Bull, Vice, Virgin, Obey, Mr. Clean, Shazam

---

## Evocative
*Borrows common words and transplants them into surprising new contexts.*

**Pros:** Familiar yet surprising, metaphorically rich, memorable
**Cons:** Risk of confusion, may feel forced if the connection is weak

**Subcategories:**
- **Metaphors** — Amazon, Apple, Square, Nest, Stripe, Robinhood, Lemonade, Safari, Acorns
- **Mythological** — Nike, Pandora, Casper, Mercury, Alibaba, Yeti, Ursa Major
- **Natural World** — Tide, Reebok, Caterpillar, Arc'teryx
- **Facet** (borrows a component/detail) — Stripe, Threads, Capsule, Medium, Hue, Segment
- **Suggestive** (hints at benefit without stating it) — Calm, Notion, Twitter, Tinder, Swiffer, Caviar

---

## Invented
*Newly minted words, crafted from existing language or sound.*

**Pros:** Completely ownable, unlimited meaning potential, strong trademark
**Cons:** Sounds like gibberish until it accumulates meaning; requires marketing investment

**Subcategories:**
- **Neologism/Coined** — Google, Kodak, Spotify, Shopify, Duolingo, Glossier, Venmo, Zappos, Kleenex, Xerox
- **Spelling Play** — Lyft, Flickr, Tumblr, Canva, Vimeo, Krispy Kreme
- **Domain-Dictated** — Art.sy, Bit.ly, Calm.com, Musical.ly
- **Special Wrongness** — Liquid Death, Nothing, Teenage Engineering, Impossible Foods, The Browser Company of New York, Band of Outsiders

---

## Identity
*Rooted in origin — person, place, cultural lineage.*

**Pros:** Personal, human, authentic, rooted in story
**Cons:** Can feel limited if the company evolves beyond founder/geography; harder to globalize

**Subcategories:**
- **People** — Dell, Ferrari, Tesla Motors, Ralph Lauren, Harry's, Warby Parker, Adidas, Ben & Jerry's
- **Place** — Cisco, Patagonia, Boston Market, Tom's of Maine, Hudson Bay Company
- **Heritage/Legacy** — Old Spice, Old Navy, Liberty Mutual, Royal Enfield
- **Culture/Language** — Uber, Häagen-Dazs, Clinique, Le Labo, Novartis

---

## Suffixes & Endings Reference

Tonally, the right suffix signals scale, industry, or personality. Use to test modifiers when a word is promising but needs shaping.

**Corporate/Institutional:** Partners, Group, Associates, Alliance, Holdings, Company/Co., International
**Financial/Investment:** Capital, Fund, Ventures, Advisors, Syndicate, Management
**Professional Services:** Consulting, Solutions, Services, Institute, Bureau, Research
**Creative:** Collective, Studio, Atelier, Works, Productions, Guild
**Scientific/Technical:** Tech/Technologies, Analytics, Data, Dynamics, Systems, Lab/Labs, Innovations
**Medical/Wellness:** Medical, Care, Clinic, Wellness, Life, Specialties
**Industrial/Supply:** Industrial, Industries, Logistics, Foundry, Properties
**Retail/Consumer:** Apparel, Outfitters, Provisions, Goods, Mercantile, Market, House
**Heritage/Family-Style:** & Sons, Brothers, Heritage, House of, Prestige

## Design System Name Patterns

*Organized by conceptual territory — useful for springboarding in Phase 2*

**Natural world / celestial:** Polaris, Aurora, Sprout, Stellar, Nova, Canopy, Grove
**Materials:** Fabric, Carbon, Clay, Slate, Linen, Quartz, Cobalt
**Geometry:** Prism, Arc, Radius, Meridian, Apex, Grid
**Tools/Craft:** Chisel, Anvil, Loom, Forge, Lathe, Stitch
**Light/Energy:** Spark, Lumina, Beacon, Glow, Flare
**Architecture/Structure:** Base, Foundation, Frame, Blueprint, Scaffold, Core
**Navigation:** Compass, Atlas, Chart, Waypoint, Heading

**To avoid:** Version numbers in names (System2), purely descriptive (DesignTokens), names claimed by major OSS projects (check npm/GitHub before committing)
