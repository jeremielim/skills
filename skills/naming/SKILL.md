# Naming

Use the six-phase method adapted from Willem Van Lancker's naming framework. Act as a naming partner:
establish criteria, create range, evaluate with evidence, and help the decision-maker commit. Match
the process to the user's current state and requested scope.

## Route the Request

Choose the earliest relevant phase supported by the user's existing work. Ask only for information
that is missing from that phase.

| User state or request | Phase | Required reference | Completion criterion |
|---|---|---|---|
| No brief, vague concept, or unclear audience | 1 — Orientation | [references/phase1-orientation.md](references/phase1-orientation.md) | A specific naming brief covers type, purpose, promise, audience, feeling, tone, and constraints. |
| Usable brief but no candidate pool | 2 — Exploration | [references/phase2-exploration.md](references/phase2-exploration.md) | The pool has enough range and volume for the naming scope, with every chosen territory represented. |
| Raw or messy candidate list | 3 — Distillation | [references/phase3-distillation.md](references/phase3-distillation.md) | A reasoned shortlist remains, with strengths, weaknesses, and obvious conflicts recorded. |
| Shortlist or one-name gut check | 4 — Validation | [references/phase4-validation.md](references/phase4-validation.md) | Each finalist has evidence and an explicit legal, linguistic, domain, and user-test status. |
| Tested finalists or decision support | 5 — Commitment | [references/phase5-commitment.md](references/phase5-commitment.md) | The decision-maker has a comparable brief for every finalist and either chooses or names the unresolved blockers. |
| Chosen name, product family, or naming governance | 6 — Stewardship | [references/phase6-stewardship.md](references/phase6-stewardship.md) | Usage, story, extensions, ownership, and future review rules are documented. |

For a single-name evaluation, read both the Distillation and Validation references. For a complete
naming engagement, work through the phases in order.

During Exploration or Distillation, read the full [name taxonomy](references/taxonomy.md). During
Validation, read the [domain strategy](references/domain-strategy.md) when domain availability or
acquisition affects the decision.

## Maintain the Naming Brief

Carry these decisions across phases:

- Thing being named and its category
- Purpose and immediate promise
- Primary audience and context of use
- One dominant feeling and three tonal sliders
- Competitive territory and differentiation goal
- Non-starters, cultural constraints, and scope for future growth

Treat user-provided facts as fixed until the user changes them. Mark assumptions and unverified
availability checks explicitly.

## Evidence and Provenance

Treat this method as an adaptation of Willem Van Lancker's naming framework, not legal or market
clearance guidance. Verify time-sensitive claims—trademarks, domains, handles, competitors, and cultural
usage—from current sources, record the source and checked date, and keep their status separate from
creative judgment.

Do not state a specific trademark, company, domain, handle, price, or availability claim from memory.
When current research is unavailable or was not requested, mark that evidence `not researched` rather
than inferring a conflict or availability result.

## Calibrate the Work

Scale candidate volume and validation depth to the decision:

- Company or enduring brand: broad exploration across multiple rounds; aim for 100–250 raw candidates.
- Product, platform, or design system: aim for 30–100 candidates.
- Feature, screen, component, or campaign: aim for 10–30 candidates and favor functional clarity.

These ranges guide effort rather than override the user's requested pace. Preserve breadth before
judgment, then narrow decisively.

## Operating Rules

1. Read the active phase reference before acting.
2. Work only the requested phase unless an earlier missing decision blocks useful progress.
3. Evaluate names against the naming brief; treat personal taste and consensus as supporting evidence.
4. Separate factual checks from creative judgment and label anything not independently verified.
5. Complete the active phase only when its routing-table criterion is met.
6. End with the relevant artifact: brief, candidate pool, shortlist, validation matrix, decision
   brief, or stewardship guide.

Recommend legal counsel for final trademark clearance. Execute purchases, filings, registrations,
or outreach only when the user explicitly requests those external actions.
