# Phase 3 — Distillation

Cut the raw pool against the naming brief. Preserve interesting tension; eliminate names because of
evidence or poor fit, not because they are initially unfamiliar.

## Evaluation Dimensions

Assess each viable candidate on:

| Dimension | Question |
|---|---|
| Depth | Does it carry useful layers of meaning without becoming cumbersome? |
| Temperature | Does its warmth or distance match the desired feeling? |
| Voice | Is it fluent, pronounceable, and distinctive aloud? |
| Visual | Does it remain recognizable in relevant sizes, cases, and interfaces? |
| Differentiation | Does it occupy useful competitive territory and avoid obvious conflicts? |
| Special wrongness | Is an unexpected quality memorable and defensible rather than merely awkward? |

Then apply the **MMR test**:

- **Memory** — Can people recall and spell it after one encounter?
- **Meaning** — Does it evoke the intended associations and emotional result?
- **Range** — Can it grow with the named thing?

Keep candidates that are strong on at least two MMR axes unless a hard constraint eliminates them.
For trademark, competitor, domain, handle, slang, or translation claims, use current sources and record
them as preliminary checks, not final clearance. When research is unavailable or outside the requested
scope, mark the check `not researched`; do not infer an external fact from memory.

## Shortlist Size

- Enduring company or master brand: 12–15 viable names.
- Product, platform, or design system: 8–12 viable names.
- Feature, screen, component, or campaign: 5–8 viable names.

If the shortlist is smaller, return to Exploration. If it is much larger, raise the bar rather than
carrying weak options forward.

## Output

Produce a comparison table with name, territory/type, strongest case, honest weakness, MMR result,
and preliminary conflict status. Summarize the main reasons other candidates were eliminated.

Phase 3 is complete when the calibrated shortlist contains only defensible candidates and every
candidate has documented strengths, weaknesses, and obvious-conflict status.
